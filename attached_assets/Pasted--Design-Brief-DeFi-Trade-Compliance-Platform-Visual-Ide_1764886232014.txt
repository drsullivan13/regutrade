# Design Brief: DeFi Trade Compliance Platform
## Visual Identity & User Experience

---

## Design Philosophy

You're designing for institutional finance professionals who exist at the intersection of two worlds: the precision of traditional finance and the innovation of decentralized technology. This isn't consumer crypto. This isn't retail trading. This is compliance officers, hedge fund traders, and RIA portfolio managers who need to **prove** their decisions to regulators.

**Core Design Principle:** *Trust through transparency. Sophistication through simplicity.*

The interface should feel like it was designed by someone who understands Bloomberg Terminal but isn't afraid to leave the 1990s behind. Clean, modern, data-dense where it matters, but never overwhelming. Every element should justify its existence.

The quiet confidence of having the right tool for a complex job. Like a surgeon's scalpel, a pilot's HUD, or an architect's blueprintâ€”precision instruments that inspire trust through their clarity.

## Visual Language

### Color Philosophy

**Primary Palette: Financial Trust**
- **Indigo/Royal Blue (Primary):** Authority, stability, intelligence. This is your action colorâ€”buttons, links, highlights. Think Morgan Stanley blue meets modern SaaS. Not the garish electric blue of consumer crypto, but the refined blue of institutional trust.
- **Slate Gray (Neutral):** The workhorse color. Backgrounds, secondary text, borders. Should feel like brushed aluminum or high-end office furniture. Never flat black (too harsh), never pure white (too stark).
- **Forest Green (Success):** When execution goes well, this should feel like a checkmark on an audit report. Subtle. Professional. Never neon.
- **Amber (Warning):** For acceptable but suboptimal execution. The color of caution lights and yellow legal padsâ€”attention without alarm.
- **Crimson (Alert):** Rarely used. Only for genuine problems. When you see red, something needs immediate attention.

**Why No Dark Mode (Yet):**  
Compliance officers work in offices with overhead lighting, not gaming setups with RGB. Documents get printed. Screens get photographed for presentations. A light, clean interface photographs well and translates to paper. Dark mode can come in v2.

### Typography Hierarchy

**Headings:**  
Strong, authoritative sans-serif. Medium weight (not boldâ€”we're confident, not shouting). Generous spacing between heading and content. Think "Federal Reserve press release" not "crypto startup blog."

**Body Text:**  
Highly legible. 16px minimum for primary content (never make finance professionals squint). Line height generous enough for scanning (1.6-1.7). Letter spacing slightly increased for maximum clarity.

**Data/Numbers:**  
Tabular numerals (numbers align vertically). Slightly heavier weight than body textâ€”numbers are the heroes here. Monospace for addresses and transaction hashes to show they're technical identifiers.

**Microcopy:**  
Lighter weight, slightly smaller, but never cryptic. Every helper text should reduce anxiety, not create it.

## Layout & Information Architecture

### The Grid System

Work in **8-point grid.** Everything snaps to multiples of 8. This creates visual rhythm and makes the interface feel intentional, not arbitrary.

**Spacing Scale:**
- 8px: Tight relationships (icon to label)
- 16px: Related elements (form fields, list items)
- 24px: Section breathing room (between cards)
- 32px: Major section breaks
- 48px: Page-level separation

### White Space as a Design Element

Information density is good. Visual clutter is not. The difference is intentional white space.

**Key Principle:** Give important information room to breathe. A single number in space is more powerful than ten numbers crammed together. The recommended route should feel like it's been elevated, given importance through spacing and containment.

## Page-by-Page Design Intent

### Page 1: Analysis & Execution

**Overall Feel:** Control center. Everything you need to make a decision is here, organized logically.

**Hero Section: Trade Input**
- Clean, spacious form at the top

- **Row Design:** Each route is a contained unit

  - Route name: Bold, larger (18px)
  - Numbers: Tabular alignment, clear hierarchy
  - The "Best?" column: Simple checkmark in a circle for recommended route, empty for others
  - Execute buttons: Right-aligned, consistent width, primary color for recommended, outline style for alternatives

- **Table Headers:**
  - Sticky when scrolling (should it get that tall)
  - Slightly smaller uppercase text with letter spacing
  - Subtle background differentiation from rows
  - Optional tooltip icons for metrics that might need explanation

**Recommendation Panel**
- Appears below table
- Left border accent in primary blue (6px)
- Light blue background tint
- Icon: Checkmark or shield (trust symbol)
- Typography: Statement should be bold and clear
- Rationale in normal weight below
- This should feel like a stamp of approval

**Execution Modal/Panel**
- Slides in or appears as overlay
- Darker background overlay (reduce visual noise from rest of page)
- Clean white modal centered
- **Progression Visualization:**
  - Vertical timeline on left showing steps
  - Each step has icon, label, status indicator
  - Active step pulses gently
  - Completed steps show checkmark
  - Pending steps are grayed out
  
**Status States:**
- Connecting wallet: Spinner with "Connecting..." text
- Approval needed: Warning icon with clear CTA
- Approving: Progress indicator

**Transaction Link:**
- Monospace font for hash

**Compliance Statement:**
- Bottom section
- Full width
- Light gray background

### Page 3: Trade History

**Feel:** Archive. Ledger. Permanent record.

**Table Design:**
- Alternating row colors (very subtleâ€”just enough to guide the eye)
- Compact but readable rows
- Sortable columns with clear indicators

  - Blue: Good  
  - Amber: Acceptable

**Interaction:**
- Click entire row to view details (not just a button)

**Feel:** Official document. Printable. Auditor-ready.

**Layout:**

**Sections:**

**Print Styling:**

## Micro-interactions & Animation

**Philosophy:** Animation should explain, not decorate.

### Button Presses
- Subtle scale down (0.98x) on active
- Shadow reduction to show "press"
- Color darkens slightly
- 150ms duration

### Loading States
- Skeleton screens where content will appear
- Spinners for actions (not page loads)
- Progress bars for known-duration tasks
- Pulse animation for awaiting external input (wallet)

### Success Feedback
- Checkmark appears with slight bounce
- Background flashes green tint momentarily (200ms)
- Optional: Subtle confetti burst (very restrained!)

### Route Comparison
- Rows fade in sequentially (100ms stagger)
- Recommended route slides in from left slightly
- Numbers count up if showing deltas

### Transaction Confirmation
- Circle fills as confirmation blocks pass
- Final confirmation: Scale up briefly, then settle

### Hover States
- 200ms transition on background color changes
- Cursor changes to pointer for interactive elements
- Scale transforms (1.02x) for cards that open details
- Border color transitions for input focus

**Golden Rule:** Every animation should complete in under 300ms. Users should never wait for UI flourish.

## Accessibility & Inclusive Design

**Color Blindness:**
- Never rely on color alone
- Use icons + text labels
- Red/green distinction supported by symbols (âœ“ and âš )

**Keyboard Navigation:**
- Tab order logical
- Focus states clearly visible (2px outline, primary color)
- All actions accessible via keyboard
- Modal trap focus when open

**Screen Readers:**
- Semantic HTML
- Labels on all inputs
- Status updates announced
- Table headers properly associated

**Contrast:**
- WCAG AA minimum (4.5:1 for body text)
- AAA preferred for primary content (7:1)
- Never gray text on light gray background

## States & Feedback

### Loading States

**Skeleton Screens:**
- Gray rectangles where content will appear
- Pulse animation (subtle)
- Correct aspect ratios for final content
- Appears immediately (no delay)

**Spinners:**
- Use sparingly (only for actions, not page loads)
- Centered in container
- Size proportional to container
- Optional: Progress percentage if known

### Error States

**Inline Validation:**
- Red border on input with error
- Error message appears below input
- Icon (âš ) before error text
- Appears immediately on blur, not on keystroke

**Page-Level Errors:**
- Banner at top of page
- Red/amber background
- Clear description of problem
- Suggested action if available
- Dismissible but persistent until resolved

**Transaction Failures:**
- Modal remains open
- Error clearly stated
- Link to troubleshooting if complex
- "Try Again" button prominent
- "Cancel" option available

### Success States

**Subtle Confirmation:**
- Checkmark icon
- Brief message
- Green accent
- Auto-dismiss after 3 seconds OR
- Persistent with manual dismiss for important actions

**Major Success (Trade Executed):**
- Full-screen takeover briefly
- Large checkmark or celebration icon
- Key metrics displayed
- CTA to view details
- Auto-transition to post-trade analysis after 2 seconds

## Design System Components

### Buttons

**Primary Action:**
- Background: Primary color (blue)
- Text: White
- Padding: 16px 32px
- Border radius: 8px
- Shadow: 2px
- Hover: Darken 10%
- Active: Press down visual
- Disabled: Gray, 50% opacity

**Secondary Action:**
- Background: White
- Text: Gray-700
- Border: 1px solid gray-300
- Same padding/radius as primary
- Hover: Gray-50 background
- Active: Gray-100 background

**Tertiary/Text Button:**
- No background
- Text: Primary color
- Underline on hover
- Used for less important actions

- Left border: 4px solid green
- Background: Green tint (5% opacity)

**Clear, Not Clever:**
- "Trade executed successfully" (yes)
- "Ka-ching! You're all set!" (no)

**Informative, Not Instructional:**
- "Connecting to Base network" (yes)
- "Please wait while we connect to Base network" (noâ€”UI already shows progress)

**Confident, Not Boastful:**
- "Execution quality: 99.48%" (yes)

### Button Labels

**Action-Oriented:**
- "Analyze Trade" not "Submit"

**Status Updates:**

## Edge Cases & Empty States

### No Wallet Connected

**Message:** "Connect your wallet to analyze and execute trades"  
**CTA:** "Connect Wallet" button (large, centered)  
**Visual:** Icon of wallet or connection symbol  
**Tone:** Encouraging, not blocking

### Wrong Network

**Message:** "Please switch to Base L2"  
**CTA:** "Switch Network" button (triggers wallet prompt)  
**Visual:** Warning icon  
**Tone:** Helpful, not scolding

### No Routes Available

**Message:** "No routing options found for this token pair"  
**CTA:** "Try Different Pair" button  
**Visual:** Search/magnifying glass icon  
**Tone:** Neutral, not error

### No Trade History

**Message:** "Your trade history will appear here"  
**CTA:** "Analyze Your First Trade" button  
**Visual:** Empty table/ledger illustration  
**Tone:** Inviting

### Transaction Failed

**Message:** Clear description (e.g., "Transaction rejected" or "Insufficient gas")  
**CTA:** "Try Again" and "Get Help"  
**Visual:** Alert icon  
**Tone:** Supportive, troubleshooting

## Progressive Disclosure

**Show What Matters, Hide What Doesn't**

**Trade Input:**
- Simple form initially
- Advanced options behind "Advanced" expander
- Slippage tolerance: Default 0.5%, editable if needed
- Deadline: Default 20 minutes, editable if needed

**Route Details:**
- Summary row shows key metrics
- Click row to expand full route details
- Route path, pool addresses, fee tiers in expanded view

**Compliance Report:**
- Summary view shows key facts
- "Show Full Report" expands all sections
- Each section collapsible individually
